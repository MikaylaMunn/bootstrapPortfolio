About me

Prior to incarceration I was attending Manchester University, studying to become a teacher. I was a volunteer for Special Olympics, Relay for Life, and after school programs for kids. I was the captain of the volleyball team, a resident-assistant and a student. I also worked during breaks and vacations at the local Tolson Center. I was even a supervisor over the Summer Camp during summer break However, I got into a very difficult time and ended up coming to prison.

During my prison stay, I ended up doing two programs, one in the kitchen for In2Work program and another in the Last Mile program. I have learned skills in both areas that have helped me become a better version of myself. I enjoy learning and being able to help others succeed in their goals. I enjoy working hard and constantly challenging myself to do something better. I understand I can learn something new daily, and even if I struggle I can always ask for assistance.
